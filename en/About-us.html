About the department

â€‹The department of journalism and language communication is quite young compared to other similar structural subdivisions. The department is headed by a candidate of philological sciences, associate professor Sikorska Victoria Yuriyivna. Initially, the department was founded in January 2020 as the pre-university training department within the faculty of work with foreign students of the Odessa National Medical University, and later it was renamed the department of journalism and language communication. By its educational, methodological, scientific, organizational, and theoretical-applied activities, the department initially provided language training for foreign students of the Odessa National Medical University and foreign citizens to enter institutions of higher education in Ukraine, but today it conducts training for specialists of full-time and part-time forms of education of the entire Odessa National Medical University and prepares future specialists in the specialty 061 "Journalism". The department is a graduating department in the direction of training specialists according to the educational and professional program "Journalism and social communications" and aims to provide in-depth knowledge and practical skills in journalism. The task of the department is to contribute to the creation of a competitive generation of journalists who want to implement their own information, social, and scientific projects, as well as to realize the importance of their role in the modern media space of Ukraine and the world. Disciplines taught by the department staff:

"Ukrainian language (by professional orientation)";
"Modern Ukrainian language of the media";
"Ukrainian language as a foreign language";
"English language (by professional orientation)";
"Business English language";
and specialized disciplines according to the educational program "Journalism and social communications".

The department performs a scientific planned state budget topic "Intercultural communication: analysis and development strategies in the context of globalization and socio-cultural transformations" (scientific supervisor - candidate of philological sciences, associate professor Sikorska V. Yu.), which covers the study of intercultural communication through the prism of development strategies of information and communication technologies in the context of globalization and analysis of the interaction of cultural communities in the modern socio-cultural space. The scientific activity of the department is implemented through systematic scientific publications and reports of the staff at international and all-Ukrainian scientific and practical conferences. The department has a scientific journal category B (035 Philology) "Black Sea Philological Studies".
